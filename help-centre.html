📚 Frequently Asked Questions
🔍
How do I calculate my CGPA using the calculator? +
Enter each course with its unit load and the grade you scored. The calculator automatically multiplies grade points by units, sums them up, and divides by total units to give your CGPA. Make sure your total units are between 18–26 for a standard semester.
What is the LASU grading system? +
LASU uses a 5-point scale: A = 5.0 (70–100%), B = 4.0 (60–69%), C = 3.0 (50–59%), D = 2.0 (45–49%), E = 1.0 (40–44%), F = 0.0 (0–39%). Your CGPA is calculated by dividing total quality points by total units attempted.
Can I save my semester data for later? +
Currently, data is saved to your browser's local storage. We're working on account-based saving so you can access your records across devices. For now, you can download your results as Excel, PDF, or Word.
How does the Grade Predictor work? +
Input your current grades and remaining courses. The predictor calculates the best and worst possible CGPA outcomes based on different grade scenarios, helping you set realistic targets.
What should I do if my calculated CGPA seems wrong? +
Double-check that you've entered the correct unit loads and grades. Carryover courses should be included. If the issue persists, use the Bug Report tab in "Your Voice" or contact me directly through the form below.
Is LASU GradeTracker official or affiliated with LASU? +
No — LASU GradeTracker is an independent student-built tool created by Ocheme Faith Ochanya to help fellow Lasuites. It is not officially affiliated with Lagos State University, but all calculations follow LASU's official grading policy.
