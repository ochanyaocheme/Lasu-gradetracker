📖 Our Story

LASU GradeTracker wasn't born from a problem I had. It was born from a vision — a burning desire to leave something behind that matters. Something that will still be here in 2 years, 5 years, 10 years. Something generations of Lasuites will look back on and be glad existed.

I didn't just wake up with the name. It took months of brain-cracking, of asking myself what I could build that no one has ever done. Something different. Something real. Something that would outlive me.

I started building this during my exam period — one of the hardest seasons of my life. I was juggling my writing project, school exams, reading, and my health because I wasn't feeling well. My eyes hurt from staring at screens late into the night and early morning. My body ached. There were moments I questioned everything: "Why are you even doing this? Why don't you just stop?"

But I didn't stop. There was a drive inside me — one I couldn't even explain — that wiped out procrastination entirely. I worked through a four-month holiday, pouring every ounce of energy into this. It got so frustrating at times that I had to step away, but I always came back. Because this isn't just a project. It's a legacy.

And it's not just about grades. It's about community. A space where Lasuites can just be — themselves, free, unjudged. Where you can vent, connect, find study partners, share confessions anonymously, jot down your feelings, and take on weekly challenges. Where academics meet humanity. Where no one is left behind.

"I didn't build this because I had a problem. I built it because I wanted to solve problems I hadn't even seen yet. For people I hadn't even met."
— Ocheme Faith Ochanya

Hey there! I'm Ochanya — a Chemistry student at LASU who believes passion beats major every single time. I don't code because it's my course. I code because I have something to say, something to build, and a community to serve.

  • Born from vision, not necessity. I didn't have carryovers. I didn't struggle with CGPA calculations. I simply saw a gap — a space where Lasuites needed more than grades. They needed each other.
  • Built through the fire. Exams. Projects. Illness. Sleepless nights. Eye strain. Body aches. Every doubt whispered at 3 AM. I pushed through because the vision was louder than the pain.
  • A community, not just a calculator. From the Lounge to the Diary, from Confessions to Study Buddies — every feature was designed so Lasuites can be free, be heard, and be themselves.
